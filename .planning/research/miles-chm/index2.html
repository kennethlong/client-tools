Welcome To Miles 9

With this new version, we've continued our focus on the sound authoring and interation process, while putting more independence in the hands of the sound designer. The highlight of this release is the replacement of the Miles 8 authoring tool with the Miles Sound Studio 9. Dan rewrote this tool from the ground up to be easier, cleaner, faster, and more powerful. He literally started at "int main()" on this one, and "dog-fooded" the whole process with his side project game, Firestorm. Anything that made life difficult or awkward for Dan was smoothed out and optimized - we're really happy with the workflow now!

We've also put more work and platforms into the runtime, adding a slew of features. High level variables make their entrance in this version, as well as fully asynchronous data loading in the event system. Banish IO hitches!

If you have used Miles Studio 9, please look at the Upgrading To Miles Studio 9.2 section for things to know for migrating for this version.

If you are new to Miles 9, but familiar with the Miles 8 event system, then you should head over to Migrating From Miles 8 to get a sense of what's going to have to change.

If you're new to all of this, head over to the Getting Started section.
