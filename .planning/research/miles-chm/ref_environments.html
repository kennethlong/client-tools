Environments are groups of settings that affect all sounds.

  • Room type - sets the global EAX-style room reverb (which is just a name for a specific combination of reverb time, predelay, and damping values). To hear the reverb effect, you must also apply a sound preset that has a non-zero wet level!

  • Reverb time, Reverb predelay, Reverb damping - sets the global reverb, overriding the room type selection if any. To hear the reverb, you must also apply a sound preset that has a non-zero wet level!

  • Reverb wet volume - attenuates the final wet (reverberated) volume of all voices. Decibels.

  • Reverb dry volume - attenuates the final dry (non-reverbed) volume of all voices. Decibels.

  • Master volume - attenuates the final output volume of all voices. Decibels.

  • 3D rolloff factor: exaggerates the distance falloff of your 3D sounds. This can be used to make things seem farther away, or it can be used to convert the units of the position functions from meters to some other space.

  • 3D Doppler factor: exaggerates the Doppler effect resulting from the source and listener's relative 3D velocity. This can be used to make the Doppler effect more pronounced, or it can be used to conform to your application's velocity units.
