Q: How do I refer to a specific High-Level Sound Instance at run-time?

A: When you have a sound instance playing (that was triggered from a sound event), then often you will need to directly control it. Normally, you would just have the sound designer create another sound event to do control it (matching with a label query), but this isn't always expressive enough.

Miles gives you several different ways to find and control sound instances:

  • First off, as you enqueue each sound, you can pass a chunk of data, or a pointer to a chunk of data, with each event. Then, when you enumerate the playing sound instances, you can look at this data to figure out which sound is which.
  • Another option is that whenever you enqueue an event, you are returned a "queued ID" that you can use to refer to any sound instances that were started by that event. Note that one sound event might start multiple sound instances, so you might have to enumerate the sounds once to get the "instance ID" (which is unique per sound instance). But, if you know your sound designer only starts one sound per event, then you can just keep the queued ID.

In either case, you usually want to do processing on the sound inside the AIL_enumerate_sound_instances loop, so that all your sound processing is isolated in one place. It's also convenient, since this is how you can get the HSAMPLE, if you want to 3D position it or do other non-event-driven sound processing.
