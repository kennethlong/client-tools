The Miles high level tool is targeted towards as much of a hands-off workflow for the programmer as possible. The basic idea is that everything related to the control of audio passes through named Events. These events are then filled out by a sound designer to affect the soundscape of your game.

The big new thing in Miles 9 is all of the testing occurs in the high level tool. You can now capture events from the game and replay them without having the game loaded. This is all covered in the User's Guide for Sound Designers.

The rest of this document is focused on the code side of Miles, and how to handle specific features.
