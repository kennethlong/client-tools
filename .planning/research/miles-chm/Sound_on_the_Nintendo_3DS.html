For sound support on the Sony 3DS, Miles interfaces to nn::snd.

Miles pre-mixes all of its audio together and then passes it down in one audio channel. For example, when you play multiple sound effects with Miles, it will still just use a single output sound channel. This sound mixing step also does sound format conversion, so you can use whatever frequency and format of audio in your Miles files, and we will automatically convert it to format that you opened Miles with (in AIL_open_digital_driver).

This conversion and mixing uses CPU, of course, and we don't have a lot to begin with, so in general, try to use the same sound rate as you opened Miles with.
