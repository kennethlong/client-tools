Overview

Moves an existing ramp to a new target over time.

  1. The name of the ramp, selected from the current Settings.
  2. The time, in seconds, the ramp takes to arrive at it's target.
  3. The target value. If you assign a variable target, it will continue to poll the variable's value until the ramp time completes.
  4. The ramp interpolation method, determining whether the movement towards the target is constant(Linear), mostly at the tail end of the timeframe (Exponential), or divided between the earlier and the late stages of the timeframe (S-Curve).

Ramps allow you to smoothly interpolate four different aspects of a sound: Volume, Wet Level, Low Pass Cutoff, and Playback Rate. The target value's unit type is dependent on the ramp type, dB, dB, Hz, and semitones, respectively.
