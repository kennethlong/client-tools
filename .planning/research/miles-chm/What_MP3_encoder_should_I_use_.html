Q: What MP3 encoder should I use?

A: The encoders are being upgraded so quickly that it's hard to give a recommendation that will be valid for any length of time, but we use LAME now, pretty much exclusively.

The Fraunhofer encoders can sound a bit better, but LAME is just more convenient to use and extend.

If you will be doing looping or seeking of any type on MP3 files, then you'll want to use our MakeLoop tool. It makes perfect loops by stretching the sound, running LAME without the bit-reservoir, and then snipping off the initial silences frames. It's super handy.

If you want to compress the DLS instruments in your files, then you need our encoder - it is the only one with the hooks necessary to encode DLS files. Our encoder isn't the best for general MP3 encoding, though.
