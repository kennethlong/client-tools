Welcome to the New Miles! We're really happy with this release, focusing on the high level tool. It has been rewritten from the ground up to be more usable, scalable, functional, and fast. These User's Guides are targeted towards sound designers, or those wanting an introduction to the tool itself. For introduction to the coding side of things, please look at the Programmer's Introduction help file.

This is the first of a series of user's guides designed to introduce you to important concepts. They will be organized in consistent manner:

  1. A brief description of the goals of the guide.
  2. A list of terminology that is assumed from prior guides, with links to definitions.
  3. A list of new terminology, with explanation.
  4. A series of steps to follow along with to learn the concept.

Requisite Terminology
None!

New Terminology

  1. Asset: Assets are single objects you edit and work with in Miles Studio. Each asset is a single file on disk. There are four core types of Assets: Events, Sounds, Presets, and Environments. All content in Miles Studio are Assets.

  2. Sound Bank: Sound banks are just a collection of Assets. When your game ships, each sound bank will be a single compiled file that is loaded very fast.

  3. Event Asset: Events are tiny script-like Assets that do all of the heavy lifting in Miles Studio. They are comprised of Event Steps, which are activated in sequence, doing a variety of things. The most common Event Step is the Start Sound Step.

  4. Timeline: The timeline is a representation of played or playing events. It is viewed on the Timeline Tab. The timeline stores a great deal of information about the event, including what sounds were started, how long they lasted, the volumes of the sounds, and what affected the volume over time. In addition, the timeline can replay old recordings.

  5. Capture: A capture is a timeline recorded from a game. These can be saved to disk and loaded in the Timeline Tab, allowing you to adjust your content and hear it without loading the game.

Steps

  1. Click Open and select media/examples.mssbnk.



    This loads a previously saved Sound Bank. Along the left of Miles Studio you will see the Assets in this sound bank.

  2. Switch to the Timeline Tab.

  3. Click Open and select media/example.msscap.

    This loads a previously saved Capture. The timeline now shows several events that were recorded and when they happened.

  4. Click Play.

Right now you are hearing a replay of a timeline I put together using the same example sound bank, but on a completely different machine. You can see the events, when they occurred, and if you click on each instance, you can see many details about them. Feel free to click around and see what all you can inspect.
