Discussion

This filter is used to add a swirling property to a sound that it is applied to, thickening the sound. An audio signal that is to be processed with chorus is first delayed by a small amount. (Typical delay times are small, in the range of 5 to 40 milliseconds.) This produces a doubling effect. Each delayed signal is then sent to a low frequency oscillator. The LFO takes the delayed signal and moves its pitch up and down, changing the tuning from sharp to flat. (The oscillator moves in the range of 1-20 cycles per seconds to get the effect.) The LFO usually runs at a slow speed; 1 to 20 oscillations per second are typical. The output of the delayed and pitch-altered signal is then mixed in with the original audio. This blending completes the chorusing effect.

Name for use with AIL_find_filter: "Chorus Filter"

Properties:

"Chorus Feedback": Regeneration amount. Range: 0.09 to 0.9 %.

"Chorus Rate": Rate of delay change. Range: 0.01 to 20.0 cycles per second.

"Chorus Depth": Depth range. Range: 0.01 to 2000.0 milliseconds per second.

"Chorus Delay": Delay of chorus signal. Range: 0.0 to 1000.0 millisecond.

"Wet Level": Volume level of the processed data. Range: 0.0 (no effect) to 1.0 (full effect).

"Dry Level": Volume level of the original data. Range: 0.0 (no effect) to 1.0 (full volume).

When a mix value of 0.0 is specified, the filter will operate on an internal "fast path" designed to consume minimal CPU resources.
