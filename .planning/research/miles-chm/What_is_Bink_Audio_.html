Q: What is Bink Audio?

A: Bink Audio is a new format designed just for the Miles Sound System. It is the audio format that Bink Video uses for sound tracks and it is very efficient. It was designed specifically for games.

Bink Audio is nice because:

  • it is super-fast to decode (on some platforms, twice as fast as MP3, and three times as fast as Ogg),
  • has competitive compression ratios (8-12 to 1),
  • is natively VBR (nice for dialog),
  • uses very little memory,
  • supports multi-channel sounds natively,
  • doesn't have the IP constraints like MP3.

It's a great format for games, and we expect it will be the default for most Miles users.

For performance comparisons to other codecs where you can see how much better it is, see this FAQ.

You create Bink Audio files with either the Miles Sound Studio, or by using the BinkA_encode command-line utility.
