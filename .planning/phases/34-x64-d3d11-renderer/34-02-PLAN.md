SHADER-CACHE cwd COLD-COMPILE (review #4 ‚ÄĒ Cursor, code-grounded): `ConfigDirect3d11.cpp:57`
hardcodes `ms_shaderCacheDir = "stage/shader-cache/"` (a RELATIVE path). Launching the exe with cwd =
`stage-x64/` resolves it to `stage-x64/stage/shader-cache/` ‚ÄĒ a COLD cache, NOT the warm 32-bit one.
So the FIRST x64 boot does a full D3DCompile pass ‚Üí noticeably LONGER startup. **Do NOT mistake the
cold-compile delay for a hang / TDR / link failure.** The compiled bytecode is arch-neutral, so for a
FAIR A/B you MAY seed the x64 cache from the 32-bit one (copy `stage/shader-cache/*` ‚Üí
`stage-x64/stage/shader-cache/`) before the timed boot; otherwise just expect the first boot to be slow
and the second fast.

`pixel X Y -e EID`, `debug pixel X Y -e EID --trace`, `export-rt`.

EID PORTABILITY WARNING (review #2 ‚ÄĒ Cursor HIGH; the single most actionable fix): **EIDs are
capture-LOCAL event indices. They do NOT correspond across two separate `.rdc` files** ‚ÄĒ even for the
identical scene, the x64 capture's EID 412 is NOT the 32-bit capture's EID 412. Matching draws by EID
number across captures diffs UNRELATED draws and can sign off a FALSE pass. **Match by SEMANTIC draw
identity instead** (see Task 2): the bound PS/VS entry-name from `shader`, the texture bindings from
`pipeline`, and `draws --filter` to isolate the dot3/bump/terrain/FFP draw class; then record each
draw's SIGNATURE (shader hash + RT format + topology) and compare pixels at a FIXED screen coordinate.

A/B DIVERGENCE TOLERANCE (review #2 ‚ÄĒ Opus MEDIUM; legit SSE-vs-x87 LSB rounding, RESEARCH ¬ßA3):
  - `shader` (compiled bytecode): BYTE-IDENTICAL expected (the HLSL compile is arch-neutral). A
    bytecode delta is a real flag.
  - `debug pixel ‚Ä¶ --trace` (per-pixel constant/output): ‚Č§1 ULP delta on float outputs is PASS
    (x64 SSE vs x86 x87 differs in the LSB ‚ÄĒ this is expected, not a regression).
  - `export-rt` (RT image): VISUALLY identical (no perceptible color/structure delta). A >1-LSB
    per-channel delta on a flat region is a flag; isolated LSB dither is PASS.
A divergence WITHIN tolerance is PASS; a divergence BEYOND tolerance is captured with the specific
pixel/constant delta and flagged (D-01 fix-what-breaks).
